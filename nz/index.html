Sandfly Bay Teaser

Sandfly Bay is a sandy bay with large dunes 15 kilometres (9.3 mi) east of central Dunedin, New Zealand. Located on the southern side of Otago Peninsula, between Seal Point and the Gull Rocks on the western side of Sandymount,[1] Sandfly Bay is a Department of Conservation wildlife reserve frequented by trampers. (Source: Wikipedia)

Sandfly Bay... what a view

Sandfly Bay is approximately 1.5 kilometres (0.93 mi) across when measured in a straight line between the headlands. On the western side the cliffs of Seal Point rise directly out of the sea in a near-straight wall about 900 metres (3,000 ft) long; beyond Seal Point along the coast lies Boulder Beach. On the eastern side lies a 1.1 kilometres (0.68 mi) white sand beach, facing south-southwest, crossed by the mouth of Morris Creek flowing in from the north. Towards the eastern end of the bay the dunes rise to over 100 metres (330 ft) above the shoreline, forming the lower slopes of Sandymount. The beach ends in a rocky headland, with an assortment of small islands known as the Gull Rocks standing out of the sea off its southern point.[1] The largest of these is known as Lion Rock or Lion's Head Rock, due to its shape. A distinctive landmark, it can be clearly seen in good weather from Second Beach, close to Saint Clair, 18 kilometres (11 mi) to the west. East of this headland the Otago Peninsula coastline turns northeastward at Harakeke Point.[1] The bay is accessible from Seal Point Road or a walking track from Sandymount. (Source: Wikipedia)
